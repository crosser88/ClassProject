Maj7 Scale Intervals

I recently got back into learning music theory again. After getting past your commonly heard major chords, it's time to spice it up.
Major chords are made up of triads, scale formula (Root, 3, and 5), the degrees of the major scale (e.g. A maj: A, B, C#, D, E, F#, G#)
As the name of the chord suggests, this is simply adding the 7th degree of the major scale to the chord (Root(A), 3(C#), 5(E), 7(G#)).
With the guitar in standard tuning, the open A string, middle finger on the 2nd fret of the D string, index finger on the 1st fret
of the G string, ring finger on the 2nd fret of the B string, and the open high E string will give you a A maj7 chord.
The chord can be played else where on the neck but may require different finger positioning.
Congratulations, you've learned how to play an A major seventh chord.
